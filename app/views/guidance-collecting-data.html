Guidance

Data to collect

We require the collection of open space data to allow for consistent comparison and analysis, by Government, the Planning Inspectorate, neighbouring authorities and other interested parties.

What open space data you should provide

Local authorities should provide data about their current population and population forecasts, current supply of different types of open space, future supply of different types of open space, and the amount of each type of open space per 1,000 of the population.

Current supply and demand

Population

You should use current population figures as a baseline. These can be obtained from the ONS population figures for the planning authority's boundary.

You should take demographics into consideration when analysing the availability of age-specific facilities, such as children’s playgrounds.

Open space

Measurement and benchmarking

Hectares (ha) within the Local Planning Authority area of each typology, split between total amount and space which is in public or private ownership.

Future supply and demand

Future supply

Use the above typologies to calculate new developments that have been granted planning permission and include new open space for the next 5 and 10 years.

Future demand

Use population projections for the next 5 and 10 years to calculate future demand.

Availability

Where benchmarks exist, analyse the amount of open space available per 1,000 of population, using the following calculation:

(total hectares of open space / total population) x 1000 = hectares of open space per 1,000

Availability deficit or surplus

Deficit or surplus for each typology, for the current year, five years from now and ten years from now, should be calculated using the following calculation:

Open space typology benchmark - hectares of open space per 1,000 = +/- surplus/deficit of open space per 1,000

Outputs should be provided using the schema below:

Typology

Enter the type of open space that the site belongs to. See the list above for the relevant typologies.

HectaresCurrent

Enter the total amount of open space currently measured.

HectaresFiveYears

Enter the total amount of open space forecast to be in place in five years.

HectaresTenYears

Enter the total amount of open space forecast to be in place in ten years.

HectaresPer1000PeopleCurrent

Enter the result of the calculation:

(total hectares of open space / total population current) x 1000

HectaresPer1000PeopleFiveYears

Enter the result of the calculation:

(total hectares of open space five years / total population five years) x 1000

HectaresPer1000PeopleTenYears

Enter the result of the calculation:

(total hectares of open space ten years years / total population ten years) x 1000

SurplusDeficitCurrent

Enter the result of the calculation:

Open space typology benchmark - hectare of open space per 1,000 current

SurplusDeficitFiveYears

Enter the result of the calculation:

Open space typology benchmark - hectare of open space per 1,000 five years

SurplusDeficitTenYears

Enter the result of the calculation:

Open space typology benchmark - hectare of open space per 1,000 ten years

How to provide open space data

Create a file from scratch

Please provide your data in a CSV file. This file should include:

  • a header row – this must be the first row in the spreadsheet, with the following headers, written exactly and with no spacing (headers are case sensitive): OrganisationURI, Typology, Reference, Name, GeoY, GeoX, Hectares, Geometry, OwnershipStatus, FirstAddedDate, LastUpdatedDate, EndDate
  • 1 row for each open space site

Use our template

We've created an open spaces data CSV template. If it helps, you can use this example file and enter your own data, following the guidelines below.

How to complete your CSV file

For each column header this is what you fill in for each open space site.

Column headers:

OrganisationURI

Find your organisation in this list and enter the corresponding Open Data Communities URI.

Typology

Enter the type of open space that the site belongs to.

This should be one of the following:

  • parks_gardens - Parks and gardens
  • natural_seminatural - Natural/Seminatural
  • outdoor_sports - Outdoor sports facilities
  • amenity - Amenity
  • children_young_people - Children and young people
  • allotments_gardens_farms - Allotments, gardens and farms
  • churchyards_cemeteries - Churchyards and cemeteries
  • civic_spaces - Civic spaces

Please take care to enter the typology exactly as it appears above.

Reference

Enter the unique reference your organisation uses to identify the open space.

If one doesn’t exist, you need to create one. It can’t be used by your organisation to identify any other sites, but it can be borrowed from another data set listing the site.

Name

Enter the name your organisation uses for the site.

GeoY

Enter the latitude of a point close to the centre of the site. The value should be 6 or fewer decimal places, using the WGS84 or ETRS89 coordinate systems specified by the open standards for government guidance.

GeoX

Enter the longitude of a point close to the centre of the site. The value should be 6 or fewer decimal places, using the WGS84 or ETRS89 coordinate system specified by the open standards for government guidance.

Make sure you don’t mix up the latitude (Geo Y) and longitude (Geo X) values. Any location in the UK will have a latitude (Geo Y) from about 49 to 57 and a longitude (Geo X) from about -7 to 2.
Hectares

Enter the land area of the site in hectares, up to 2 decimal places. Use digits (2) rather than words (two).

Geometry

If you know the shape of the site, enter it using the WKT representation. You can publish polygons derived from Ordnance Survey.

OwnershipStatus

Indicate the site’s ownership by entering one of the following values:

  • public_ownership - owned by a public authority
  • not_public_ownership - not owned by a public authority
  • mixed_ownership - mixed ownership

Please take care to enter the ownership status exactly as it appears above.

FirstAddedDate

Enter the date that the site was first added to this register, in the format YYYY-MM-DD.

LastUpdatedDate

Enter the date this entry in the register was updated, in the format YYYY-MM-DD.

EndDate

If the site no longer needs to be listed (for example, if the site has been built on), it should stay on the register for historical reasons. Enter the date the site was developed or determined to no longer be an open space, in the format YYYY-MM-DD. This field should only be filled in if the site is no longer classified as open space.
